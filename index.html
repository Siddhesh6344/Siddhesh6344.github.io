Research

My research focuses on the intersection of AI, Computational and Systems Neuroscience, with the goal of developing algorithms that uncover interpretable computational principles of brain function. I am particularly interested in learning structured representations from neural data (e.g., EEG, iEEG, fMRI, fUSI and fNIRS) to understand how information is encoded and processed in the brain. My recent work explores self-supervised learning, sparse representations, and multimodal neural data modeling to build models that can reveal latent neural mechanisms rather than only making predictions. More broadly, I aim to develop Neuro-inspired AI systems and mechanistically interpretable learning frameworks that bridge biological intelligence and AI, enabling new approaches for understanding brain dynamics, cognition, and next-generation neurotechnology.
